# 0046 — grok-4.5 promoted to build.standard and validate; executor routing extended to validate

- Status: accepted
- Date: 2026-07-08
- Amends: ADR-0030 (default bindings table), ADR-0031 (rote-only delegation rubric),
  ADR-0040 (drive as build-path-only variant)

## Context

SpaceXAI released Grok 4.5 (2026-07-08: $2/$6 per Mtok, 500K context) the same day
grok CLI 0.2.91 retired the `grok-build` model id, breaking the `build.rote`
binding. Before re-deciding bindings, we built a re-runnable bakeoff harness
(`eval/`) and ran a 180-cell matrix: 12 build units (8 replay tasks carved from
shipped landings with behavioral oracles + 4 traps re-arming ADR-0031's recorded
failure modes) and 12 validate scenarios (6 clean, 6 planted defects that keep
suite and lint green), grok-4.5 vs Sonnet 5 (build incumbent) vs Opus 4.8
(validate incumbent), 3 reps, pre-registered rubric, contamination-controlled
fixtures with canary scanning (zero leaks).

## Evidence (eval/results/full-2026-07-08, decision-memo.md there)

- **Build**: grok-4.5 passed every integrity trap 3/3 — including the red-test
  trap that confined grok-build to rote — with zero suppressions, zero undisclosed
  excursions, zero false-success-on-truncation, and it *finished* the oversized
  truncation probe under caps that timed sonnet out 2/3. Replay pass-rate 95.8%
  (sonnet 100%; the one miss is a 1-in-5 variance flake the drive verification
  bar catches by design). Cost per passed task ~$0.01 estimated vs sonnet's $1.94
  reported; 1.7m vs 4.0m mean wall.
- **Validate**: the only false-passes from ANY model were the lint-suppression
  scenario — grok 3/3, sonnet 2/3, **opus (incumbent) 1/3**. The contract said
  "run lint" but never that a suppression is disqualifying; models papered over
  the gap with cultural judgment, unreliably. With one added integrity-gates
  paragraph, grok recovered 3/3 with findings recall 1.0 and zero false-passes
  across 36 tuned cells. Its remaining false-fails traced to an eval-fixture
  artifact (the baseline normalization leaked into judged diffs), not the model.
  Otherwise grok: zero false-fails on clean scenarios, recall within band of
  opus, ~3x faster, ~100x cheaper.

## Decision

1. `build.standard` and `validate` bind to `grok-4.5` via the `grok` executor
   (Jackson, 2026-07-08, on the evidence above; economics decisive).
2. The execution pipeline's executor routing extends to validate: an
   executor-bound `validate` binding spawns the drive agent (shared
   `executorReroute`), which owns assembly and landing while the executor judges
   judge-only and is mechanically gated (drive.md "Validate briefs").
3. `agents/validate.md` gains the integrity-gates paragraph — suppressions,
   weakened/deleted tests, and non-biting tests are defects on their own. This
   lands regardless of binding: the incumbent leaked suppressions 1-in-3 without
   it.
4. ADR-0031's rote-only delegation rubric is superseded by evidence-gating:
   tiers with recorded eval evidence route silently (`rote`, `standard`,
   `validate`); `build.complex` remains off-rubric until evaluated.
5. `validate` keeps opus reachable as a one-line revert
   (`"validate": { "model": "opus" }` in settings) — the eval harness re-runs on
   demand if grok regresses.

## Consequences

- Anthropic-token spend concentrates in `plan` (session), `build.complex`
  (opus), and `drive` (sonnet, now supervising three routed roles).
- grok applies contracts literally (playbook lore): judgment norms must be
  spelled out in briefs — the harness's tuned-contract experiment is the
  template for closing future gaps.
- Known caveats ride the record: grok cost figures are transcript-estimated (its
  CLI reports no usage); the corpus is n=12 per leg (gates and order-of-magnitude
  gaps are meaningful, single-digit deltas are not); `grok trace` uploads
  remotely unless `--local` (one eval session trace was uploaded before this was
  known).

## Rejected alternatives

- **Sonnet for validate** (cost middle-ground): failed the same hard gate
  (2/3 suppression false-passes) at ~80x grok's cost.
- **Grok-specific prompt fork** for the suppression gap: the gap was the shared
  contract's, and the incumbent leaked through it too — fixed once for every
  model instead.
- **Waiting for the tuned-kernel incumbent arms to finish**: Jackson called it —
  the marginal evidence wouldn't change either binding, and the artifact-affected
  fixtures needed rework first.
